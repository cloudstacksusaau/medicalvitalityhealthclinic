One of the ways that the cells in your body communicate with each other is by releasing certain hormones. Aging may create severe hormone imbalances that may contribute to depression, inflammation, osteoporosis, coronary artery blockage, and loss of libido. These age-related hormone imbalances can affect quality of life. As testosterone, estrogen, progesterone (in women), and thyroid hormone levels inevitably decline….. mood, energy levels, muscle mass, metabolism, memory, mental performance, and a fulfilling sex life can all go by the wayside. Age-related declines in hormone levels can be extreme. Serious health problems often don’t get complete care, because many doctors fail to recognize that some of their aging patients’ disorders may be caused by hormone concentrations that are up to 90 % lower than they used to be.
